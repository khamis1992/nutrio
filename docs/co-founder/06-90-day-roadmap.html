Understanding LTV:CAC Ratio

LTV (Lifetime Value) = Total money a customer spends with us from signup until they stop subscribing.

CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost) = Money we spend to get each new customer (marketing, ads, referral programs).

LTV:CAC Ratio = How efficient our marketing is. For example, 56:1 means for every 1 QAR spent on marketing, we get 56 QAR back in customer value.

Industry Standard: 3:1 is considered good. Anything above 10:1 is excellent.

Our 56:1 ratio is exceptionally strong because it shows customers find high value in our service, and we're acquiring them efficiently through referrals and word-of-mouth (not expensive ads).
