## Value Proposition Presentation (10 minutes)

### The Concept
"Here's how Nutrio Fuel works:

Customers pay us a monthly subscription fee - ranging from 299 to 1,299 QAR depending on how many meals they want per week. This gives them access to meals from ALL our partner restaurants, including yours.

Instead of paying per meal, they pay one flat fee. We handle everything - marketing, customer acquisition, payments, delivery logistics. You just focus on what you do best: making great food."

### The Financials
"For every meal you prepare through our platform, we pay you a fixed amount - typically between 20-30 QAR per meal. This is paid weekly, directly to your bank account.

Let me show you what this could look like:

If you serve just 20 meals per day through our platform:
- 20 meals × 25 QAR = 500 QAR per day
- 500 QAR × 30 days = 15,000 QAR per month
- That's 15,000 QAR in additional revenue with:
  - Zero marketing costs
  - Guaranteed customers
  - Predictable income"

### Benefits Summary
"Here's what you get as a partner:

✓ **Guaranteed Revenue** - Predictable weekly income
✓ **Zero Marketing Costs** - We bring the customers
✓ **Higher Volume** - Subscription model drives consistent orders
✓ **No Commission** - Unlike delivery apps that take 15-30%
✓ **Brand Exposure** - Reach health-conscious customers
✓ **Flexibility** - Set your own daily capacity limits
✓ **Support** - Dedicated WhatsApp support channel
✓ **Weekly Payouts** - Automatic bank transfers every Monday"

## Objection Handling

### "We're already on Talabat/Deliveroo"
**Response:** "That's great - it means you're already set up for delivery. But here's the difference: Those platforms charge you 15-30% commission on every order, and you're competing with hundreds of other restaurants.

With Nutrio Fuel:
- You get a fixed payout per meal (no percentage)
- You're not competing - subscribers choose from ALL partners
- We handle marketing so you don't have to
- Our customers are specifically looking for healthy meals - better match for you"

### "What if we get too many orders?"
**Response:** "You have complete control. You can set a daily capacity limit in your dashboard. Once you hit that limit, you stop receiving orders for the day. You can also temporarily pause orders if you need to."

### "How do we know we'll actually get orders?"
**Response:** "That's a fair concern. Here's our commitment: [If offering guarantee] We guarantee a minimum of X orders per week for your first month. If we don't deliver, we pay the difference. Plus, you can leave the platform with just 30 days notice if it's not working out."

### "Our food is premium/expensive"
**Response:** "Perfect! That's exactly what our customers want. They're paying a subscription because they value quality healthy meals. You're not competing on price - you're being paid for the quality you deliver."

### "We don't want to compromise on quality with high volume"
**Response:** "We don't want that either. That's why you control your daily capacity. You'll never receive more orders than you can handle while maintaining your standards. Quality is how you get good ratings, which leads to more orders."

### "The payout seems low compared to our regular prices"
**Response:** "I understand. Let's look at the full picture:
- Your regular price: [X] QAR
- Minus delivery app commission (20%): [X] QAR
- Minus packaging: [X] QAR
- Minus marketing costs: [X] QAR
- Your net: [X] QAR

With us:
- Fixed payout: 25 QAR
- No commission
- No marketing costs
- Guaranteed customers
- Plus volume from subscription model

Many partners find they actually earn more per meal with us because of the volume and lack of fees."

## Next Steps

### If They're Interested
"Great! Here's what happens next:

1. **Today:** I'll send you our partnership agreement to review
2. **This week:** You fill out our online application (takes 15 minutes)
3. **Next week:** We do a quick kitchen visit and menu setup
4. **Week 2:** Training session (30 minutes)
5. **Week 3:** You go live on the platform!

Do you have any questions before we move forward?"

### If They Need Time
"I completely understand - this is a new model. Take a few days to think about it. 

Can I send you:
- Our partnership agreement
- Some case studies from similar restaurants
- A sample of what your dashboard would look like

When would be a good time for me to follow up?"

## Red Flags to Watch For

### Operations
- [ ] Unclean kitchen
- [ ] Unreliable staff
- [ ] Can't meet capacity requirements
- [ ] Poor packaging

### Attitude
- [ ] Not enthusiastic about partnership
- [ ] Unwilling to meet standards
- [ ] Focused only on price
- [ ] Difficult to communicate with

### Food Quality
- [ ] Poor taste
- [ ] Inconsistent quality
- [ ] Doesn't match healthy positioning
- [ ] Bad reviews online

If 2+ red flags: Consider declining partnership.
