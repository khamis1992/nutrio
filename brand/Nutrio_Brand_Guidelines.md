# Nutrio Brand Guidelines

Version: 1.0  
Status: Production brand source of truth  
Primary product: Nutrio mobile application  
Primary logo reference: `public/logo.png`

## 1. Brand Foundation

Nutrio is a modern wellness and nutrition platform that helps people eat better, stay active, and build healthier daily routines without friction. The brand combines healthy meals, nutrition tracking, gym and activity access, personalized goals, AI guidance, and positive lifestyle change into one calm, motivating mobile experience.

Nutrio must not feel like a generic food delivery app, a harsh fitness tracker, or a clinical health system. It should feel like a premium daily companion: friendly, intelligent, fresh, reliable, and easy to use.

### Purpose

Make healthy living easier to start, easier to understand, and easier to maintain.

### Mission

To help customers make better daily nutrition and activity decisions through convenient meals, clear progress, personalized recommendations, and supportive wellness experiences.

### Vision

To become the most trusted mobile wellness platform in Qatar and the region for healthy meals, nutrition intelligence, gym access, and personalized lifestyle progress.

### Brand Promise

Nutrio turns healthy choices into simple daily actions.

### Personality

- Friendly: warm, human, and approachable.
- Modern: clean, mobile-native, and current.
- Intelligent: helpful recommendations without complexity.
- Motivating: progress-focused, positive, and energizing.
- Fresh: bright food imagery, healthy colors, and airy composition.
- Premium: polished, consistent, refined, never cheap or loud.
- Supportive: never shaming, never aggressive, never fear-based.
- Reliable: clear states, trustworthy data, and predictable interactions.

### Values

- Balance over extremes.
- Progress over perfection.
- Clarity over complexity.
- Personalization over generic plans.
- Accessibility over exclusivity.
- Trust over hype.
- Convenience over friction.
- Real food over artificial wellness language.

### Target Audience

Primary users:

- Busy professionals who want healthy meals without planning friction.
- People managing weight, energy, protein intake, or nutrition goals.
- Gym members and active users who need meal guidance around training.
- Customers who want a premium but approachable wellness app.
- Arabic and English speakers in Qatar who expect local context, clear delivery, and mobile-first UX.

Secondary users:

- Restaurant partners.
- Gym and wellness partners.
- Admin and operations teams.
- Coaches and nutrition professionals.
- Marketing and social media teams.

### Market Positioning

Nutrio sits between healthy meal delivery, personal nutrition coaching, activity support, and lifestyle progress. It is more intelligent than a standard delivery app, more approachable than a medical nutrition tool, and more food-centered than a typical fitness tracker.

### Key Differentiators

- Healthy meals connected to goals.
- Personal nutrition and activity context.
- AI-powered but human-readable guidance.
- Arabic and English support.
- Partner ecosystem for restaurants, gyms, and wellness services.
- Progress visualizations inspired by the logo's circular motion and segmented balance.
- Mobile-native experience built around daily actions.

### Emotional Experience

Nutrio should make users feel:

- "I can do this."
- "Healthy choices are easier here."
- "The app understands my routine."
- "My progress is visible."
- "This feels premium but not intimidating."
- "I trust the recommendations."

Avoid creating feelings of guilt, fear, pressure, confusion, or clinical diagnosis.

## 4. Logo System

The Nutrio logo combines a bowl, leaf, circular movement, checkmark, segmented color path, and bold wordmark. These elements represent food, health, progress, balance, completion, and positive lifestyle movement.

### Logo Elements

- Bowl: healthy meals, nourishment, practical food choices.
- Leaf: freshness, natural ingredients, wellness, growth.
- Circular movement: daily routine, continuity, progress tracking.
- Checkmark: achievement, trust, completed goals, confidence.
- Colored segments: nutrition variety, activity, hydration, energy, personalization.
- Deep navy shape: structure, reliability, premium trust.
- Wordmark: approachable strength, clarity, confidence.

### Logo Variations

Primary logo:

- Symbol plus Nutrio wordmark.
- Use for splash screens, marketing, onboarding, website, formal partner materials, and brand documents.

Logo symbol:

- Bowl, leaf, checkmark, and segmented ring without wordmark.
- Use for app icon, favicon, compact UI branding, social avatar, and small partner badges.

Horizontal logo:

- Symbol left, wordmark right.
- Use in website headers, email headers, and partner documents.

Vertical logo:

- Symbol above wordmark.
- Use in splash screens, posters, onboarding, and centered brand moments.

App icon:

- Symbol only.
- Must be centered with generous padding.
- Use a white or very soft background unless a platform-specific adaptive icon requires otherwise.

Monochrome version:

- Use deep navy on light backgrounds.
- Use white on dark navy backgrounds.
- Use only when color reproduction is limited.

Dark-background version:

- Symbol and wordmark in white with controlled color accents only if contrast is strong.
- Avoid placing the full-color logo on busy dark photos.

Light-background version:

- Primary full-color logo.
- Preferred background: white, soft background, or pale neutral.

Small-size version:

- Use symbol only under 80px width in digital UI.
- Do not use the full logo in tiny navigation, badges, or dense tables.

Favicon:

- Symbol only.
- Avoid wordmark.
- Maintain contrast and centered padding.

### Clear Space

Use the height of the checkmark circle as the minimum clear space around the full logo. For the symbol, use 20 percent of the symbol width as minimum clear space. No text, border, image, or UI element should enter this space.

### Minimum Size

Digital:

- Full logo minimum width: 120px.
- Horizontal logo minimum width: 140px.
- Symbol minimum size: 28px.
- App icon minimum display size: 32px.
- Favicon: 16px, 32px, 48px exported versions.

Print:

- Full logo minimum width: 28mm.
- Symbol minimum width: 8mm.

### App Icon Padding

- Symbol should occupy 68 to 74 percent of the icon canvas.
- Keep at least 13 percent padding on all sides.
- Never crop the segmented ring.
- Avoid extra outlines, glows, or random container shapes.

### Placement Rules

- Place the logo on calm backgrounds.
- Prefer top-left in LTR layouts and top-right in RTL layouts when used in headers.
- Use centered placement for splash and onboarding.
- Use symbol-only for repeated UI surfaces.
- Do not repeat the full logo across every screen.

### Incorrect Logo Usage

Do not:

- Stretch, squeeze, rotate, skew, or distort the logo.
- Change proportions between symbol and wordmark.
- Recolor individual logo parts.
- Add heavy shadows, bevels, glow effects, or outlines.
- Place on low-contrast backgrounds.
- Crop the bowl, ring, leaf, or checkmark.
- Recreate the wordmark with another font.
- Place the logo inside arbitrary shapes.
- Use the full logo in very small UI spaces.
- Use the logo as a decorative pattern.

## 5. Logo Usage Rules

The logo should signal brand ownership, not decorate every surface. Use logo fragments and brand principles in the product instead of repeating the full mark.

Approved product usage:

- Splash screen.
- Login and onboarding.
- App icon.
- Empty states when brand reassurance is useful.
- Partner co-branding.
- Report headers.
- Email and notification templates.

Avoid:

- Logo in every card.
- Logo as a watermark behind body text.
- Logo in every button.
- Logo repeated in loading states.
- Full logo in bottom navigation.

## 9. Typography

Nutrio typography must be mobile-readable, friendly, premium, and multilingual. It should support English, Arabic, mixed language, nutrition units, prices, numbers, and dates.

### Recommended Fonts

Primary Latin: Plus Jakarta Sans  
Primary Arabic: IBM Plex Sans Arabic or Noto Kufi Arabic  
Fallback: system UI, Arial, sans-serif  
Numeric emphasis: Plus Jakarta Sans, tabular numbers enabled when possible

If a single cross-language font is required, use IBM Plex Sans Arabic for Arabic UI and Plus Jakarta Sans for English UI. Avoid condensed fonts and decorative scripts.

### Typography Rules

- Use sentence case for most UI text.
- Use uppercase only for short labels like "PROTEIN" or "TODAY".
- Arabic labels should not use artificial letter spacing.
- Use tabular numbers for metrics and prices.
- Units should be smaller and secondary: `520 kcal`, `38g protein`, `1.2L water`.
- Do not use font sizes below 11px in mobile UI.
- Avoid all-caps Arabic.

## 10. Shape Language

Nutrio shape language comes from the logo: circular arcs, segmented rings, organic curves, soft asymmetry, leaf forms, and progress paths.

### Core Shape Principles

- Rounded containers with meaningful hierarchy.
- Circular progress and segmented arcs.
- Organic masks for food and lifestyle imagery.
- Leaf-inspired curves for empty states and success moments.
- Segmented color accents that suggest variety and balance.
- Soft asymmetric background forms, never random blobs.

### Usage

Mobile headers:

- Use curved lower edges, layered circles, or subtle arc fragments.
- Avoid generic rectangular hero cards.

Progress indicators:

- Prefer segmented rings, arcs, and progress paths over identical stat cards.

Background decoration:

- Use faint arcs or leaf fragments at low opacity.
- Do not use unrelated decorative orbs.

Meal sections:

- Food image should carry visual weight.
- Nutrition tags may attach to image edges or sit in layered groups.

AI Coach:

- Use purple/sky segmented paths to express intelligence and personalization.

Achievements:

- Use circular badge forms with controlled energy gradients.

Bottom navigation:

- Active state should feel like a small circular segment or soft capsule inspired by the logo ring.

## 11. Iconography

Nutrio icons should be rounded, simple, consistent, and legible at small sizes.

### Style

- Stroke width: 2.2px for standard 24px icons.
- Rounded caps and joins.
- Use outline icons by default.
- Filled icons may be used for active states, achievements, and large category symbols.
- Minimum touch icon target: 44px container.
- Minimum visible icon size: 18px.
- Standard icon size: 22px to 24px.
- Feature icon size: 28px to 36px.

### Color Rules

- Inactive: Secondary Text.
- Active: semantic feature color.
- Disabled: Disabled Text.
- Error: Coral plus label.
- Success: Turquoise/Green plus label.

### Icon Meanings

- Meals: bowl, fork/knife, plate, leaf.
- Calories: flame, energy ring.
- Protein: muscle, egg, protein strand.
- Water: droplet.
- Gym: dumbbell, access pass.
- Activity: footprint, pulse, running.
- AI Coach: sparkle, neural path, chat bubble.
- Progress: ring, trend, checkmark.
- Achievements: medal, trophy, badge.
- Subscription: crown, pass, wallet.
- Delivery: bag, route, scooter.
- Profile: rounded user.
- Settings: sliders or gear.
- Notifications: bell.

Avoid mixing emoji-style icons, sharp enterprise icons, and inconsistent third-party icon packs on the same surface.

## 12. Illustration

Nutrio illustration should feel premium, fresh, and friendly, not childish.

### Style

- Organic geometry.
- Smooth curves.
- Simplified people only when needed.
- Leaf and bowl-inspired elements.
- Circular progress forms.
- Healthy food shapes.
- Limited depth and soft lighting.
- Clean backgrounds.
- Controlled Nutrio accent colors.

### Usage

- Onboarding: explain daily routine, meals, activity, progress.
- Empty states: gently guide next action.
- Errors: calm, clear, non-alarming.
- Success: celebrate without confetti overload.
- No meal selected: food-forward illustration.
- No gym access: pass or gym doorway illustration.
- Completed goal: circular achievement badge.
- Subscription expired: helpful renewal visual.
- Offline state: simple connection path.
- AI Coach: purple/sky intelligent guidance motif.

Avoid random stock illustrations, childish cartoons, or medical diagrams.

## 13. Photography

Photography must make Nutrio feel real, fresh, and premium.

### Meal Photography

Use:

- Natural color.
- Fresh ingredients.
- Visible texture.
- Balanced composition.
- Bright but not washed-out lighting.
- Clean backgrounds.
- Realistic portions.
- High resolution.

Avoid:

- Dark, low-quality food photos.
- Over-saturated meals.
- Heavy filters.
- Cluttered backgrounds.
- Fake stock-food styling.
- Extreme closeups that hide the meal.

### Lifestyle and Fitness

Use:

- Real gym environments.
- Natural movement.
- Diverse people.
- Positive energy.
- Qatar-relevant spaces when possible.
- Wellness moments, not extreme transformation imagery.

Avoid:

- Unrealistic body standards.
- Aggressive bodybuilding tone.
- Medical clinic imagery unless explicitly required.
- Stock photos that feel generic.

### Restaurant and Partner Materials

- Show real partner spaces.
- Use clean food preparation shots.
- Keep partner branding secondary to Nutrio when inside Nutrio UI.
- For co-branding, preserve both identities without color conflict.

## 14. Image Treatment

Images should not all live inside identical rectangles. Nutrio image treatment should feel layered, mobile-native, and food-forward.

Approved treatments:

- Organic masks.
- Rounded asymmetric crops.
- Edge-to-edge food hero images.
- Layered nutrition labels.
- Soft overlays for readability.
- Floating metric groups.
- Subtle color-tinted image backgrounds.
- Gradient scrims only when text must sit on images.

Avoid:

- Same card treatment for every image.
- Heavy shadows on every photo.
- Dark overlays that hide food quality.
- Low-contrast text on imagery.
- Cropping food so tightly it becomes unclear.

Nutrio is mobile-first. Desktop and tablet views should extend the mobile experience, not replace it with desktop dashboards.

- 360px
- 375px
- 390px
- 414px
- 430px

### Spacing

|---|---:|---|

- One primary action per screen zone.
- Avoid dense two-column forms on mobile.

- Use horizontal scrolling only for short chip sets or media carousels.

## 16. UI Principles

Nutrio UI must be:

- Mobile-native.
- Thumb-friendly.
- Easy to scan.
- Personal and motivational.
- Visually distinctive.
- Accessible.
- Calm but energetic.
- Built around daily progress.

### Permanent UI Rules

- Start screens with a clear focal point.
- Use one dominant accent per screen.
- Use navy for structure and text.
- Use turquoise for positive action and progress.
- Use food images prominently where relevant.
- Use custom progress visuals instead of default stats cards.
- Use bottom sheets and native-feeling controls on mobile.
- Give empty states a clear next action.
- Keep admin/partner portals related but more utilitarian.

### Avoid

- A generic dashboard grid.
- Too many white cards.
- Card inside card.
- Default Shadcn appearance.
- Desktop layouts compressed into mobile.
- Random accent colors.
- Too many gradients.
- Unlabeled icons.
- Small text.

## 17. Component System

Each Nutrio component must define purpose, anatomy, variants, states, spacing, color, typography, accessibility, and RTL behavior.

### App Header

Purpose: orient the user and provide primary screen actions.

Anatomy:

- Back or menu action.
- Screen title.
- Optional status label.
- Optional trailing action.

Variants:

- Standard.
- Large title.
- Transparent over image.
- Sticky.
- Modal header.

Rules:

- Back arrow flips in RTL.
- Title truncates after one line.
- Touch targets minimum 44px.
- Do not crowd more than two actions.

### Bottom Navigation

Purpose: access primary destinations.

Anatomy:

- 4 or 5 destinations.
- Icon.
- Label.
- Active indicator.
- Optional badge.

Rules:

- Active state uses a circular or segmented Nutrio-inspired highlight.
- Inactive icons are neutral.
- It must not cover content.
- Works in RTL and LTR.

### Buttons

Purpose: make actions clear and thumb-friendly.

Variants:

- Primary: turquoise background, white or deep navy text depending contrast.
- Secondary: white surface, navy text, subtle ring.
- Tertiary: muted surface, navy text.
- Text: no container, semantic color.
- Destructive: coral.
- Icon button: 44px minimum.
- Floating action: use rarely for key creation actions.
- Compact action: 36 to 40px only when surrounded by non-critical UI.
- Full-width mobile action: 52 to 56px height.

States:

- Default.
- Pressed: scale 0.98 or darker surface.
- Focused: visible ring.
- Hover: web only.
- Disabled: reduced opacity plus disabled text.
- Loading: spinner and label.
- Success: temporary checkmark.
- Error: message below or toast.

Avoid:

- Multiple primary buttons in one decision area.
- Small tap targets.
- Unlabeled icon-only actions when meaning is unclear.
- Excessive gradients.

### Inputs and Forms

Purpose: collect information with low friction.

Fields:

- Text.
- Search.
- Password.
- Phone.
- OTP.
- Weight.
- Height.
- Date.
- Goal.
- Slider.
- Stepper.
- Segmented control.
- Checkbox.
- Radio.

Rules:

- One primary field per row on mobile.
- Labels are always visible.
- Use correct keyboard type.
- Inline validation near the field.
- Bottom sheets for selections.
- Full-screen flows for complex forms.
- Keyboard-aware action area.
- Do not use desktop-style dropdowns.

### Meal Item

Purpose: show meal identity, image, macro relevance, and action.

Anatomy:

- Food image.
- Meal type.
- Meal name.
- Restaurant.
- Key macro or calories.
- Action.

Variants:

- Compact list.
- Schedule item.
- Detail header.
- Selected meal.
- Unavailable meal.

Rules:

- Food image should be prominent.
- Do not repeat nutrition if a full nutrition section follows.
- Selected state must be visually clear and removable.

### Nutrition Progress

Purpose: show progress without creating dashboard clutter.

Preferred forms:

- Segmented ring.
- Arc.
- Thin stacked bar.
- Daily timeline.
- Macro group.

Avoid four identical statistic cards unless comparison is the core task.

### AI Recommendation

Purpose: explain smart guidance.

Rules:

- Use purple/sky accent.
- Explain why, not only what.
- Never claim medical diagnosis.
- Show confidence or data source when relevant.
- Provide one clear next action.

### Gym Access Component

Purpose: connect activity, partner access, and recovery meals.

Rules:

- Use activity/sky/turquoise accents.
- Show booking/access state clearly.
- Tie back to recovery or nutrition only when useful.

### Toasts

Purpose: brief confirmation or error.

Rules:

- Short text.
- Icon plus status.
- No long paragraphs.
- Respect RTL.
- Error toasts should not be the only error explanation for forms.

### Empty, Error, Loading, Offline States

Empty:

- Explain what is missing.
- Show one next action.
- Use friendly illustration.

Error:

- Say what happened.
- Say how to recover.
- Avoid blame.

Loading:

- Use skeletons that reflect final layout.
- Avoid indefinite spinners when content structure is known.

Offline:

- Explain connection issue.
- Preserve user work when possible.

### Protein

## 19. Motion

Motion should feel smooth, light, natural, and purposeful.

### Timing

- Tap feedback: 80 to 120ms.
- Button success: 180 to 240ms.
- Bottom sheet entry: 220 to 320ms.
- Page transition: 240 to 360ms.
- Progress count-up: 500 to 900ms.
- Achievement reveal: 600 to 1000ms.

### Easing

- Standard: cubic-bezier(0.2, 0.8, 0.2, 1).
- Exit: cubic-bezier(0.4, 0, 1, 1).
- Spring-like motion may be used for achievements and selection.

### Use Motion For

- Progress updates.
- Button feedback.
- Bottom sheets.
- Navigation transitions.
- Loading completion.
- Achievement completion.
- Meal selection.
- Goal completion.
- AI recommendation reveal.
- Success confirmation.

### Avoid

- Animation only for decoration.
- Long looping animations.
- Motion that delays task completion.
- Parallax in dense forms.
- Ignoring reduced-motion preferences.

Reduced motion:

- Replace large transitions with fades.
- Disable count-up animations when requested.
- Keep feedback instant and clear.

## 21. Arabic and English Rules

Nutrio must support Arabic RTL and English LTR structurally, not only through text alignment.

### Direction

- English: left-to-right.
- Arabic: right-to-left.
- Mixed content: preserve natural direction for names, numbers, units, and brands.

### Alignment

- Align body text to reading direction.
- Numeric metric groups may remain visually centered.
- Tables and timelines should reverse order in RTL when sequence is directional.

### Icons

- Back arrows flip in RTL.
- Forward chevrons flip in RTL.
- Icons representing physical objects do not flip unless direction matters.
- Progress rings can remain clockwise unless the product intentionally defines RTL progress.

### Bottom Navigation

- LTR order follows English navigation hierarchy.
- RTL order should mirror only if the information architecture expects it in Arabic.
- Keep labels short enough for Arabic.

### Spacing

- Add spacing between Arabic and English words in mixed labels.
- Use non-breaking spaces for values and units when possible: `38 g`, `520 kcal`.

### Text Truncation

- Avoid truncating Arabic in primary actions.
- Use two-line wrapping for important labels.
- Use tooltips or detail screens for long partner names.

### Numbers

- Support Western numerals and Arabic numerals depending locale settings.
- Keep nutrition units consistent: kcal, g, mL, L.
- Prices should follow local formatting for QAR.

## 22. Accessibility

Nutrio must be accessible by default.

### Standards

- Normal text contrast: WCAG AA minimum 4.5:1.
- Large text contrast: minimum 3:1.
- Touch targets: 44 by 44px minimum.
- Body text: 13px minimum, 14px preferred.
- Support dynamic type where platform allows.

### Requirements

- Every icon-only action needs an accessible label.
- Important statuses need text plus color or icon.
- Error messages must identify the field and issue.
- Focus states must be visible on web.
- Screen readers should receive logical labels and order.
- Images need useful alt text unless decorative.
- Motion must respect reduced-motion settings.
- Long text must wrap safely.
- RTL layouts must be tested.
- Color-blind users must not lose meaning.

## 24. Brand Applications

### App Icon

Use the symbol only, centered with generous padding. Use a calm white or soft background. Never place the full wordmark in the app icon.

### Splash Screen

Use vertical logo, Deep Navy or Soft Background, subtle motion from the segmented ring, and a short loading transition.

### Onboarding

Use large friendly illustrations, clear benefit statements, and one action per screen. Emphasize meals, goals, activity, and daily progress.

### Login

Keep simple. Use logo, clear fields, large CTA, and trust cues. Avoid busy marketing content.

### Home Screen

Lead with today's meaningful action, not a generic dashboard. Use a hero metric or meal plan moment, then supporting actions.

### Meal Plan

Food imagery must be primary. Nutrition data should be integrated, not repeated in multiple sections.

### Meal Detail

Use image hero, clear meal identity, macro summary, ingredient options, notes, and one main add/schedule action.

### AI Coach

Use purple/sky intelligence styling. Explain recommendations in simple language. Include "why this matters."

### Gym Access

Use activity and turquoise styling. Connect booking/activity to recovery meals when relevant.

### Subscription

Use premium but calm visuals. Avoid aggressive upsell. Explain value clearly.

### Delivery Tracking

Use route/timeline visuals. Provide status, ETA, and support access.

### Profile

Organize personal data into readable sections. Avoid dense settings pages.

### Notifications

Short, helpful, and action-oriented. Use positive tone.

### Empty State

Use illustration plus one next step. Avoid blank lists.

### Success State

Use checkmark, circular completion, and short celebration.

### Social Media

Use food imagery, logo symbol, turquoise accents, and clear benefit-led copy.

### Website Hero

Use real product or food imagery, not abstract generic gradients. Brand name or offer should be obvious in first viewport.

### Partner Dashboard

More utilitarian than customer app but still uses Nutrio color, spacing, and typography. Avoid dark admin command-center styling unless needed for data emphasis.

### Email

Logo header, clear subject, white surface, navy text, turquoise CTA.

### Push Notification

Friendly, concise, no shame or medical claims.

## 25. Do and Do Not

### Do

- Use calm backgrounds.
- Use Nutrio colors selectively.
- Create one strong focal point per screen.
- Use food imagery prominently.
- Use organic shapes and segmented progress.
- Use mobile-native interactions.
- Maintain clear hierarchy.
- Use custom progress visualization.
- Keep the experience positive.
- Support RTL and LTR structurally.
- Make status readable without relying on color alone.

### Do Not

- Use every brand color at once.
- Build the interface entirely from cards.
- Use generic SaaS layouts.
- Use default component-library styling.
- Create desktop screens and shrink them.
- Use inconsistent icon styles.
- Use excessive gradients.
- Use excessive shadows.
- Use tiny text.
- Use low-quality food images.
- Mix unrelated illustration styles.
- Repeat the logo throughout every screen.
- Make the brand feel clinical, aggressive, or childish.

## 26. Implementation Checklist

Use this checklist before approving any Nutrio design or implementation.

### Brand Fit

- Does it clearly feel like Nutrio?
- Does it use logo-inspired shapes, not only logo colors?
- Does it feel fresh, modern, premium, and supportive?
- Does it avoid generic dashboard patterns?

### Mobile First

- Does it work at 360px, 375px, 390px, 414px, and 430px?
- Are touch targets at least 44px?
- Does content avoid being hidden by bottom navigation?
- Are key actions thumb-friendly?

### Color

- Is there one dominant accent?
- Are neutrals doing most of the layout work?
- Are status colors paired with text or icon?
- Are contrast requirements met?

### Typography

- Is text readable on mobile?
- Are Arabic and English styles defined?
- Are numbers and units clear?
- Is long text handled safely?

### Components

- Are components using Nutrio tokens?
- Are states defined?
- Are default library styles customized?
- Is RTL behavior defined?

### Data

- Is progress visualized clearly?
- Are metrics grouped meaningfully?
- Are charts readable on mobile?
- Is color not the only communication method?

### Voice

- Is the message supportive?
- Is it non-judgmental?
- Does it avoid medical claims?

- Are screen reader labels present?

## Final Brand Review

This system is designed to feel clearly like Nutrio because it uses more than color: it uses the logo's circular motion, segmented progress, food and leaf symbolism, supportive voice, mobile-first spacing, nutrition-specific visualization, and Arabic/English structure.

It is distinctive from generic health apps because it connects meals, progress, activity, AI guidance, and partner access into one branded experience.

It is mobile-first because layout, spacing, touch targets, bottom navigation, forms, sheets, and data visualization are defined around phone widths first.

It supports Arabic and English through typography, direction, icon flipping, text wrapping, spacing, and navigation rules.

It can become reusable tokens and components because colors, typography, spacing, radius, shadows, motion, component anatomy, and states are defined in implementation-ready terms.

This document should be treated as the permanent visual and verbal authority for Nutrio unless superseded by a future approved brand system version.
