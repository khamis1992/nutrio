02 / BRAND FOUNDATION

A wellness companion, not another tracker

# Purpose

Make healthy living easier to start, easier to understand and easier to maintain.

## Mission

Nutrio helps customers make better daily nutrition and activity decisions through convenient meals, clear progress, personalized recommendations and supportive wellness experiences.

## Vision

To become the most trusted mobile wellness platform in Qatar and the region for healthy meals, nutrition intelligence, gym access and sustainable lifestyle progress.

## Core values

Balance over extremes.

Progress over perfection.

Clarity over complexity.

Personalization over generic plans.

Trust over hype.

Convenience without losing quality.

Support without shame or pressure.

## Brand personality

| **Attribute** | **What it means**                                   | **What to avoid**                             |
|---------------|-----------------------------------------------------|-----------------------------------------------|
| Friendly      | Warm, human and clear                               | Childish or overly casual                     |
| Intelligent   | Helpful recommendations with understandable reasons | Technical or mysterious AI language           |
| Fresh         | Bright food imagery and active composition          | Over-saturated or noisy visuals               |
| Premium       | Polished, consistent and refined                    | Cold, exclusive or luxurious for its own sake |
| Motivating    | Progress-focused and encouraging                    | Shame, fear or aggressive fitness language    |
| Reliable      | Predictable interactions and trustworthy states     | Ambiguous data or hidden conditions           |

03 / POSITIONING & AUDIENCE

Where Nutrio sits and who it serves

# Positioning statement

> For people who want healthier routines without overthinking them, Nutrio is a mobile wellness platform that connects healthy meals, personal goals, activity and smart guidance into one simple daily experience.

## Primary audiences

Busy professionals seeking healthy meals without planning friction.

Users managing weight, energy, protein, hydration or general wellness goals.

Gym members who want meals and recovery guidance around activity.

Arabic and English speakers in Qatar who expect local context and premium mobile UX.

## Brand territories

Healthy meal subscriptions and delivery.

Goal-based nutrition and meal scheduling.

Activity, gym access and recovery support.

AI-assisted wellness guidance.

Partner ecosystem for restaurants, gyms and wellness services.

04 / LOGO SYSTEM

Meaning, hierarchy and correct use

# Logo meaning

Bowl: nourishment, practical meals and daily choice.

Leaf: freshness, natural ingredients and growth.

Circular motion: continuity, routine and measurable progress.

Checkmark: confidence, completion and trust.

Colored segments: balance across nutrition, hydration, energy and activity.

Deep navy: stability, premium trust and strong structure.

## Approved hierarchy

| **Asset**               | **Use**                                           | **Minimum digital size**     |
|-------------------------|---------------------------------------------------|------------------------------|
| Primary vertical lockup | Splash, onboarding, formal brand materials        | 120 px width                 |
| Horizontal lockup       | Web, email and partner headers                    | 140 px width                 |
| Symbol only             | App icon, favicon, compact UI ownership           | 28 px                        |
| Monochrome              | Single-color production or restricted backgrounds | Same as corresponding lockup |
| Small-size symbol       | Dense UI where the wordmark would be unreadable   | 24-28 px                     |

## Clear space and placement

Full logo: minimum clear space equal to the checkmark-circle height.

Symbol: minimum clear space equal to 20% of symbol width.

Use calm, high-contrast backgrounds.

Use the full logo sparingly; use the symbol for repeated product surfaces.

Do not use the logo as a decorative pattern or watermark behind body text.

## Never

Stretch, rotate, skew or redraw the logo.

Recolor individual elements outside approved variants.

Add glows, bevels, outlines or heavy shadows.

Crop the ring, bowl, leaf or checkmark.

Place the full logo inside small navigation items or buttons.

Recreate the wordmark with a substitute font.

05 / COLOR SYSTEM

Recognizable, calm and accessible

| **Swatch** | **Color**       | **HEX**  | **Primary use**                        | **Accessibility note**                    |
|------------|-----------------|----------|----------------------------------------|-------------------------------------------|
|            | Deep Navy       | \#0C1222 | Primary text, structure, dark surfaces | Excellent contrast with white             |
|            | Dark Navy       | \#101A34 | Navigation, premium panels, splash     | Use white foreground                      |
|            | Turquoise       | \#20C7A5 | Primary action, health and progress    | Use navy text where possible              |
|            | Fresh Green     | \#24CBA7 | Success, nutrition and freshness       | Use navy or white depending size          |
|            | Sky Blue        | \#2BB9F3 | Hydration, information and activity    | Prefer navy foreground                    |
|            | Purple          | \#6674F4 | AI, personalization and achievements   | White on saturated surface                |
|            | Orange          | \#FF7900 | Calories, energy and urgency           | Avoid long text on white                  |
|            | Coral           | \#FF5368 | Error, warning and attention           | Pair with icon and text                   |
|            | Soft Background | \#F6F8FC | Primary app canvas                     | Deep navy text                            |
|            | Secondary Text  | \#6E7689 | Descriptions and metadata              | Do not use below 14px on pale backgrounds |

## Color behavior

Use one dominant accent and one supporting accent per screen or major section.

Let neutral backgrounds and navy typography carry most of the interface.

Never use all accent colors at once except in controlled charts or the logo.

Color never carries meaning alone: pair status with text, icon or shape.

Gradients are highlights, not the default surface treatment.

| **Semantic role**         | **Color** | **Typical use**                   |
|---------------------------|-----------|-----------------------------------|
| Primary action            | \#20C7A5  | Main CTA, progress completion     |
| Information               | \#2BB9F3  | Hydration, activity, links        |
| AI / premium intelligence | \#6674F4  | AI Coach, personalization         |
| Energy / calories         | \#FF7900  | Calories, energy, priority        |
| Error / attention         | \#FF5368  | Failures, destructive warning     |

06 / TYPOGRAPHY

Readable, bilingual and mobile-native

# Recommended families

English: Plus Jakarta Sans.

Arabic: IBM Plex Sans Arabic.

Fallback: system UI, Arial, sans-serif.

Metrics and prices: tabular numerals when available.

| **Style**     | **Size / weight** | **Use**                       |
|---------------|-------------------|-------------------------------|
| Display       | 36 / 800          | Onboarding or campaign hero   |
| Screen title  | 28 / 800          | Primary mobile title          |
| Section title | 22 / 800          | Major content zones           |
| Subheading    | 18 / 700          | Secondary hierarchy           |
| Body large    | 16 / 600          | Important supporting copy     |
| Body          | 14 / 500          | Standard product text         |
| Caption       | 11-12 / 700       | Metadata and compact labels   |
| Metric        | 34 / 800          | Primary numerical progress    |
| Button        | 14 / 800          | Primary and secondary actions |

## Rules

Use sentence case for most interface text.

Never use artificial letter spacing in Arabic.

Avoid all-caps Arabic.

Keep units secondary: 520 kcal, 38 g, 1.2 L.

Do not reduce text below 11 px; prefer 14 px for body copy.

Test long Arabic labels before approving component width.

07 / SHAPE, ILLUSTRATION & PHOTOGRAPHY

Build recognition beyond the logo

# Shape language

Segmented arcs and circular progress paths.

Leaf-inspired curves and soft organic masks.

Controlled asymmetry rather than random blobs.

Rounded edges with different radii by hierarchy, not one radius everywhere.

Open sections and layered backgrounds instead of box-heavy composition.

## Photography

Natural, bright and realistic meal photography.

Visible ingredient texture and balanced portions.

Real partner spaces and Qatar-relevant environments where possible.

Natural movement and diverse people in fitness photography.

Avoid extreme body ideals, dark stock imagery and aggressive transformation language.

## Illustration

Organic geometry, clean backgrounds and controlled Nutrio accents.

Use for onboarding, empty, error, offline and success states.

Keep people simplified and respectful; avoid childish cartoon styling.

Do not mix multiple illustration systems across the app.

08 / MOBILE UI PRINCIPLES

Mobile is the product, not a smaller desktop

| **Rule**          | **Standard**                                |
|-------------------|---------------------------------------------|
| Primary widths    | 360, 375, 390, 414 and 430 px               |
| Page padding      | 16 px small phones; 20 px larger phones     |
| Section spacing   | 24-32 px                                    |
| Touch target      | 44 × 44 px minimum                          |
| Primary button    | 52-56 px height                             |
| Inputs            | 52-56 px height                             |
| Bottom navigation | 72 px plus safe area                        |
| Primary actions   | Place within thumb reach whenever practical |

## Permanent composition rules

Start with one clear focal point and one primary action.

Do not turn every section into a card.

Use bottom sheets for selection and full-screen flows for complex tasks.

Avoid wide tables and desktop modals.

Use horizontal scrolling only for short, discoverable collections.

Ensure bottom navigation never covers content.

Design keyboard-open states, loading states and empty states from the beginning.

## Card rule

A card is allowed only when content behaves as one independent object or requires visual separation. Open sections, dividers, timelines, sheets, integrated metrics and image-led composition should be considered first.

10 / PRODUCT PATTERNS

How Nutrio should feel in key journeys

# Home

Lead with today’s status and next meaningful action.

Use one hero progress visualization instead of four equal stat cards.

Show the next meal, goal progress and one AI recommendation in priority order.

Make subscription information visible only when relevant.

## Meals

Food imagery is primary.

Use organic masks, edge-to-edge imagery or layered information.

Keep calories/macros integrated and concise.

Use bottom sheets for replacement, extras and details.

## AI Coach

Use purple/sky visual language.

Explain why the recommendation appears.

Avoid diagnosis and medical certainty.

Provide one clear next action.

## Gym access

Show access state, validity and action clearly.

Use activity/sky/turquoise accents.

Connect activity to nutrition only when useful.

11 / DATA VISUALIZATION & MOTION

Motivation without dashboard clutter

# Visualization hierarchy

One hero metric plus supporting context.

Segmented arcs and rings inspired by the logo.

Timelines for meals, habits and weekly consistency.

Thin bars for secondary values.

Readable numbers and units; never color alone.

| **Metric**       | **Preferred treatment**              | **Color**         |
|------------------|--------------------------------------|-------------------|
| Calories         | Hero ring or energy path             | Orange            |
| Protein          | Macro progress with target           | Purple            |
| Water            | Droplet progression or thin bar      | Sky Blue          |
| Weight           | Calm trend line and contextual label | Navy / Turquoise  |
| Steps / activity | Path or journey                      | Sky Blue / Orange |
| Weekly adherence | Timeline with completion markers     | Turquoise         |

## Motion timing

| **Interaction**    | **Duration** |
|--------------------|--------------|
| Tap feedback       | 80-120 ms    |
| Button success     | 180-240 ms   |
| Bottom sheet       | 220-320 ms   |
| Page transition    | 240-360 ms   |
| Progress count-up  | 500-900 ms   |
| Achievement reveal | 600-1000 ms  |

Use motion to explain state, direction and completion.

Avoid decorative looping animations.

Support reduced-motion preferences with fades and instant feedback.

12 / VOICE & TONE

Supportive, clear and non-judgmental

| **Use**                                   | **Avoid**                         |
|-------------------------------------------|-----------------------------------|
| “You’re close to today’s protein target.” | “You failed your target.”         |
| “Here’s a better match for today.”        | “This is a bad choice.”           |
| “Add a meal when you’re ready.”           | “You must log your meal now.”     |
| “Your order needs a little more time.”    | Vague or defensive delay language |

## Writing principles

Short, human and action-oriented.

Positive without motivational clichés.

No shame, fear, diagnosis or unrealistic promises.

Explain what happened and what the user can do next.

Localize meaning, not only words, between Arabic and English.

## Approved bilingual examples

| **Situation** | **English**                                              | **Arabic**                                    |
|---------------|----------------------------------------------------------|-----------------------------------------------|
| Welcome       | Welcome back. Let’s make today easier.                   | أهلًا بعودتك. خلينا نخلي يومك أسهل.            |
| Protein       | You’re close to today’s protein target.                  | أنت قريب من هدف البروتين اليوم.               |
| Delay         | Your order is taking a little longer. We’re tracking it. | طلبك يحتاج وقتًا إضافيًا قليلًا. نحن نتابعه.     |
| Error         | Something didn’t load. Try again in a moment.            | لم يتم تحميل المحتوى. حاول مرة أخرى بعد قليل. |
| Achievement   | Nice work. You completed your weekly consistency goal.   | عمل رائع. أكملت هدف الالتزام الأسبوعي.        |

13 / ACCESSIBILITY & RTL

A default requirement, not a final check

WCAG AA: 4.5:1 for normal text and 3:1 for large text.

Touch targets at least 44 × 44 px.

Every icon-only action has an accessible label.

Every status uses text or symbol in addition to color.

Errors identify the field, issue and recovery action.

Support dynamic type and reduced motion where the platform allows.

Test screen-reader order and keyboard focus on web surfaces.

## Arabic RTL

Mirror structural direction, not only text alignment.

Flip back and forward arrows when direction matters.

Review swipe behavior and timeline sequence.

Keep object icons unflipped unless direction is meaningful.

Test mixed Arabic/English names, numbers and units.

Avoid truncating Arabic primary actions.

Use wrapping and responsive component heights.

14 / DESIGN TOKENS

Single source for design and code

# Naming principles

Use semantic names in product code.

Keep raw palette values internal.

Share one token package across Figma, mobile and web.

Version tokens and document breaking changes.

## Implementation order

1\. Finalize vector logo and exact palette extraction.

2\. Create Figma variables and bilingual text styles.

3\. Publish token package.

4\. Build priority mobile components.

5\. Create approved reference screens.

6\. Add automated visual regression and accessibility checks.

7\. Store these guidelines as permanent AI project knowledge.

15 / APPLICATIONS & APPROVAL

Consistent across product and communication

| **Application** | **Primary guidance**                                       |
|-----------------|------------------------------------------------------------|
| App icon        | Symbol only, centered, calm background, generous safe area |
| Splash          | Vertical lockup, restrained ring motion                    |
| Onboarding      | One benefit and one action per screen                      |
| Home            | Today-first hierarchy, one hero metric                     |
| Meal detail     | Image-led, clear macros, one primary action                |
| Partner portal  | More utilitarian but same tokens, type and voice           |
| Email           | White surface, navy text, turquoise CTA                    |
| Social          | Real food imagery, symbol and concise benefit-led message  |

## Approval checklist

Does it unmistakably feel like Nutrio beyond color?

Was the customer mobile experience designed first?

Is there one clear focal point and one primary action?

Are default library styles fully customized?

Are Arabic RTL and English LTR both tested?

Are loading, empty, error and offline states included?

Does the implementation preserve APIs, logic and accessibility?

Has the screen passed visual review at 360, 390 and 430 px?
